1 Executive Summary

LATTITUDE is an all-in-one personal fitness platform that bridges the gap between nutrition tracking, workout logging, and overall fitness journey management. Where most fitness apps focus on a single pillar, LATTITUDE combines all three into one cohesive product — built around the philosophy that you define and have the attitude to strive towards the latitude to build the body you want.

The product currently exists as a web application focused on its first pillar: precision nutrition tracking and recipe management. It is designed from the ground up for physique-focused athletes — individuals who need accurate, gram-level macro tracking rather than rough estimates — but is built to scale toward a broader fitness audience as workout and journey tracking features are introduced.

The Name LATTITUDE is a deliberate mashup of "latitude" (the freedom to build the body you want, on your terms) and "attitude" (the mindset and consistency required to get there). It reflects a feel-good, positive, empowering approach to eating and training — not the harsh, restrictive culture common in fitness apps.

2 Problem Statement

Athletes and fitness enthusiasts who take their physique seriously currently have to use multiple separate apps to manage their complete fitness picture:

  • A nutrition tracker for food and macros (MyFitnessPal, Cronometer)
  • A workout logger for training sessions (Strong, Hevy)
  • A progress tracker for body measurements and photos
  • A recipe app or spreadsheet for managing their own meals

These tools don't talk to each other, don't share context, and require the user to manually bridge the gaps. A physique athlete preparing for a show needs to know not just "what did I eat today" but how today's nutrition aligns with their prep phase, training load, and stage timeline — all in one view.

Additionally, existing nutrition apps are built for general wellness. They are not designed for the precision required by physique athletes who cook in batches, weigh everything in grams, and need to reverse-engineer meals around specific macro targets rather than just log what they already ate.

3 Vision & Mission

Vision

To be the single platform where serious athletes manage their entire fitness journey — nutrition, training, and physique progress — with the precision of a professional and the freedom to define their own path.

6 Core Features

F1

Recipe Library Implemented

The homepage dashboard displays all saved recipes as cards. Each card shows the recipe name, emoji, tags, a color-coded macro bar, and per-serving macros (calories, protein, carbs, fat). Clicking a card navigates to a full recipe detail page.

Key behaviours

  • Filter by tag (Breakfast, Dinner, High Protein, Meal Prep, etc.)
  • Recipes are defined in recipes.js and automatically populate the grid
  • Each recipe detail page includes a serving size scaler, ingredient breakdown table, macro dashboard, and Nutrition Facts label
  • Donut ring chart shows macro split (protein / carbs / fat % of calories)

Data source

recipes.js — a central registry. New recipes are added by creating an HTML file in /recipes/ and adding one object to the array.

F2

Recipe Builder Implemented

A browser-based tool for building recipes from scratch using gram-based ingredient input. Found at builder.html under the "Builder" tab.

Key behaviours

  • Input recipe name, emoji, description, serving count, and tags
  • Add ingredients with: name, raw gram weight, and per-100g macro values (sourced from USDA or package label)
  • Each ingredient's contribution is calculated automatically: (grams ÷ 100) × per-100g value
  • Live macro dashboard in the sidebar updates in real time as ingredients are added
  • Serving scaler lets you preview macros for 1 to N servings
  • Export panel generates the recipes.js snippet ready to copy-paste

Calculation standard

All weights are raw/uncooked unless batch weighing is used. USDA FoodData Central is the recommended data source for per-100g values.

F3

Reverse Engineer Implemented

A separate tab in builder.html that works backwards from macro targets to ingredient quantities. Designed for meal planning when you know what you need to hit but not yet what to eat.

Key behaviours

  • Set daily per-serving targets: protein (g), carbs (g), fat (g) — calories auto-calculate
  • "Load my daily goals" button pre-fills targets from the saved profile
  • Add ingredients with per-100g values; solve hints appear showing: "To meet remaining 35.2g protein: use 138g — 160kcal · 35.2P · 0C · 1.2F"
  • "Use →" button auto-fills the gram field with the solved quantity
  • Right sidebar shows progress bars for each macro against target, plus a remaining/over status for each
  • Status badge: On Target / Budget Remaining / Over on a macro

Independence

The Reverse Engineer tab is fully independent of the Builder tab. They do not share state.

F4

Batch Weighing Implemented

A panel in the Builder tab that resolves the "recommended serving size" problem for home-cooked recipes. After cooking, you weigh the finished batch and the app determines accurate per-serving macros accounting for cooking yield.

Key behaviours

  • Shows raw ingredient total (sum of all ingredient gram weights entered)
  • User inputs the finished cooked batch weight
  • Cooking yield is calculated and displayed: e.g. "80.5% yield · lost 185g during cooking"
  • 3 serving size suggestions auto-generated, spread across small/medium/large portions
  • Each suggestion card shows: serving count, weight per serving, protein per serving
  • If a profile is saved, the suggestion closest to daily protein target ÷ 4 meals is badged "Best for goals"
  • Clicking a suggestion card auto-fills the serving count and updates the full macro dashboard
  • Manual override available: define by serving weight or serving count

Why batch weighing matters

Raw ingredient weights do not equal cooked weight. Chicken loses 20–30% of its weight through moisture loss during cooking. Without batch weighing, per-serving macros based on raw ingredient totals will be inaccurate for cooked foods. This feature ensures precision at the serving level.

F5

Profile & TDEE Calculator Implemented

Found at profile.html. A setup page where the user enters their physical stats and fitness context to generate personalized daily macro targets.

Inputs

FieldOptions
Unit systemImperial (default) or Metric
Weightlbs (imperial) or kg (metric)
Heightft + in (imperial) or cm (metric)
AgeNumeric input
Activity levelSedentary (×1.2) · Lightly Active (×1.375) · Moderately Active (×1.55) · Very Active (×1.725) · Extra Active (×1.9)
Prep phaseMaintenance (0) · Bulk (+300 kcal) · Cut (−400 kcal) · Peak Week (−500 kcal)

Calculations

  • BMR: Mifflin-St Jeor formula — 10×weight(kg) + 6.25×height(cm) − 5×age + 5
  • TDEE: BMR × activity multiplier
  • Calorie target: TDEE + phase adjustment
  • Protein: 1g per lb bodyweight (physique-athlete standard)
  • Fat: 0.35g per lb bodyweight
  • Carbs: remaining calories ÷ 4

Output

Saves to localStorage (offline) and Firestore (cloud sync). Displayed on the homepage dashboard as a goals bar: prep phase + daily protein / carbs / fat / calories.

F6

Cloud Sync (Firebase) Implemented

All user data syncs across devices via Firebase Firestore with Google Sign-In authentication.

Key behaviours

  • Google Sign-In button in the nav on every page
  • On sign-in: profile data loads from Firestore and is cached to localStorage
  • On save: profile writes to both localStorage (instant) and Firestore (cloud)
  • On any device: sign in with the same Google account → full profile syncs automatically
  • Offline fallback: localStorage is always used when Firestore is unavailable or user is not signed in
  • Security: Firestore rules enforce that users can only read/write their own data (request.auth.uid == userId)
