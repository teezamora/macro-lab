Instructions
  1. Place chicken in the bottom of the Instant Pot. Season well with salt and pepper.
  2. In a small bowl, combine honey, soy sauce, onion, ketchup, oil, and garlic. Pour over chicken. Seal lid and set valve to sealing.
  3. Cook on Meat setting (or HIGH pressure) for 15 minutes. Quick release when done. Remove chicken — leave sauce in pot.
  4. Dissolve cornstarch in 1/4 cup water, pour into pot, and stir to combine. Press Sauté and cook sauce ~5 minutes until slightly thickened.
  5. Slice tofu into bite-size cubes and add to the pot. Let sit in sauce for 2–3 minutes to absorb flavor.
  6. Cut chicken into bite-size pieces, return to pot, and toss everything with sauce.
  7. Divide into 5 equal portions. Garnish with sesame seeds and green onions. Serve over rice (macros above exclude rice).

⚡ Men's Physique Notes
One of the best macro profiles in the lab: 40.3g protein, 37g carbs, 13g fat at 424 kcal — that's a lean, high-protein meal with a 37.7% protein ratio. This is exactly the kind of meal that supports muscle retention while allowing you to stay lean enough to show conditioning year-round.

Dial back the honey during prep: The 32g of sugar per serving comes almost entirely from the 1/2 cup of honey. If you're 8–12 weeks out, cut the honey to 1/4 cup — you'll drop ~65 kcal and ~16g sugar per serving. The cornstarch still thickens the glaze and the garlic flavor stays strong.

Swap to low-sodium soy sauce before peak week: Soy sauce is responsible for almost all the sodium here (4,469mg out of 5,699mg total). Low-sodium soy sauce cuts that roughly in half, dropping per-serving sodium from ~1,140mg to ~580mg — a meaningful difference when you're managing water in the final 1–2 weeks before a show.

Tofu supports lean conditioning: Firm tofu adds 36.8g protein to the batch with a clean fat profile. For Men's Physique, where a narrow waist and full upper body is the look, lean protein sources like tofu that don't add excess fat help keep your conditioning tight without sacrificing the muscle fullness judges want to see.

Carb cycle around this meal: On heavy upper body training days (back/shoulder supersets, chest), eat this with a cup of white rice (~53g carbs) to maximize glycogen and muscle fullness. On rest days or lower body-only days, skip the rice and keep it as written to stay in a mild deficit.
