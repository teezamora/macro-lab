⚡ Men's Physique Notes
Offseason breakfast built for size: At 568 kcal and 35g protein, this burrito is a strong offseason meal to support muscle fullness in the shoulders, chest, and back — the areas judges score hardest in Men's Physique. Keep it in rotation during your building phase.

Too heavy for deep prep — time it right: The 28g fat and 568 kcal per burrito makes this a better offseason or early prep meal. In the final 8–12 weeks before a show, swap the sausage for turkey sausage or lean ground turkey to cut fat by roughly 10g per serving while keeping protein high.

Sodium strategy: 1,132mg per burrito is significant — fine in the offseason, but something to manage as you get closer to stage. Reduce the cheddar and skip added salt to bring sodium closer to 700–800mg when water retention becomes a factor in peak week.

Fiber keeps you stage-lean: 8g of fiber per burrito supports a clean gut, which is especially important when you're dieting down. A flat, tight midsection on stage is partly about digestive health — fiber helps.

Freeze and stay consistent: Consistency is everything for contest prep. Wrap these individually, freeze them, and reheat at 425°F for 20 min from frozen. Having 35g-protein breakfasts ready removes any excuse to go off-plan during crunch time.
